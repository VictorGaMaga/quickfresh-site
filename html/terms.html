Standards & Guidance (informative)

The following standards and technical guidance inform industry best practice on waterproofing, moisture-resistant board specification, and edge/seal protection for internal linings:

  • AS 3740:2021 — Waterproofing of domestic wet areas (referenced by the National Construction Code) — minimum requirements for waterproofing and water-resistant materials in wet areas; emphasises correct materials and detailing around junctions and wet zones.
  • National Construction Code (NCC) — Wet area provisions — requires wet-area elements to be waterproof or water-resistant and recognises AS 3740 as accepted practice.
  • AS/NZS 1859.2 — Reconstituted wood-based panels — classifies MDF by service condition (e.g., MR — moisture-resistant). For MDF skirtings/trims, MR grade or proper sealing of cut edges is recommended to reduce moisture uptake.
  • Manufacturer guidance consistently advises that cut/exposed MDF edges must be sealed/primed (primer, sealant, varnish or suitable coating) to minimise swelling and moisture damage.

Notes: These references are informative and explain why unsealed skirtings or non-MR MDF are at greater risk when exposed to incidental moisture during cleaning.
